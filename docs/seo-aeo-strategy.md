# MoreBetterLabs SEO/AEO Strategy

**Goal**: Maximize SEO rankings and qualified traffic for ruzuku.com by building morebetterlabs.com as a learning design authority hub under Abe Crystal, PhD.

**Theory of the case**: MBL is not a blog — it's an E-E-A-T engine. A PhD researcher's dedicated learning design site, with contextual editorial links to the platform he built, is exactly what Google's quality raters consider the gold standard for expertise signals. Every piece of content on MBL serves a dual purpose: (1) rank for learning design queries MBL owns, and (2) pass topical authority + link equity to Ruzuku.

## 1. Strategic Positioning: MBL vs. Ruzuku

### The Differentiation Rule

| | MoreBetterLabs | Ruzuku |
|---|---|---|
| **Angle** | "Here's what the research says" | "Here's how to do it" |
| **Voice** | Academic researcher who built a platform | Platform that helps you build courses |
| **Depth** | Learning science, theory, frameworks | Practical steps, tools, features |
| **Audience** | Course creators who want to understand *why* | Course creators who want to *do* |
| **Keywords** | "learning design", "[theory] for course creators" | "how to create", "course platform", "vs" |
| **Intent** | Informational (learn, understand) | Transactional/commercial (build, buy, compare) |

### What MBL Owns (Ruzuku Does NOT Cover)

- Learning design as a discipline (theory, research, frameworks)
- Abe's original teaching frameworks (2 Traps, Dual Minimalism, Know-Feel-Do application)

### What Ruzuku Owns (MBL Must NOT Compete With)

- "How to create an online course" (pillar page + 26 getting-started articles)

- Interactive tools (quiz, outline generator, pricing calculator, revenue calculator)

These topics exist on both sites. The rule: MBL takes the **research/theory** angle, Ruzuku takes the **practical/how-to** angle. MBL links TO Ruzuku's practical content.

## 5. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

### Why AEO Matters for MBL

AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) extract answers from authoritative sources. MBL's PhD-authored, research-cited content is exactly what these systems prioritize. If MBL becomes a source that AI search engines cite for learning design questions, every citation includes Abe Crystal's name and a path to Ruzuku.

### AEO Implementation

#### 5a. Question-shaped H2 headings
AI search engines extract content under question headings. Rewrite section headings as questions where natural:

| Current H2 | AEO-Optimized H2 |
|------------|-------------------|
| "The Instructionism Trap" | "What Is the Instructionism Trap in Course Design?" |
| "Social Presence" | "How Does Social Presence Improve Online Learning?" |
| "Measurable Criteria" | "How Do You Define Measurable Outcomes for a Course?" |
| "The Iterative Piloting Process" | "Why Should You Pilot Your Course Before Perfecting It?" |

**Rule**: Not every H2 needs to be a question. Use questions for sections that answer a specific query someone might type. Use statements for sections that are part of a narrative flow.

#### 5b. Direct-answer first sentences
The first sentence after each H2 should be a standalone answer. AI search engines extract this.

**Bad**: "There's a lot of research on this topic that we should consider..."
**Good**: "Social presence — the feeling that other people are participating alongside you — is one of the strongest predictors of online course completion."

#### 5c. FAQ sections on every article
Add a 3-4 question FAQ section at the bottom of each article. These serve dual purpose:
1. Visible content that AI search engines can extract
2. FAQ schema in JSON-LD that search engines can index

**FAQ examples for "The 2 Traps" article:**
- "What is the biggest mistake in online course design?"
- "Why do expert course creators make bad courses?"
- "How do you fix a course that students don't finish?"
- "What is the Curse of Knowledge in course creation?"

#### 5d. Article schema enhancements
Current article schema is good. Add:
- `keywords` array in Article schema
- `about` field linking to relevant schema.org concepts (e.g., `"about": {"@type": "Thing", "name": "Learning Design"}`)
- `citation` array for research references (builds credibility signal)
- `educationalLevel` field ("Professional", "Advanced")

#### 5e. Sitewide FAQ page
Create `/faq/` with the 10-15 most searchable learning design questions, each with a concise answer + link to the relevant article for depth. This page becomes a structured-data goldmine for AEO.
